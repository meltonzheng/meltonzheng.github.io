So you wanted to know more about me. I was born and raised in a suburban community east of LA near Pasadena. It was a community that was mostly Asian, so I had the pleasure of a highly competitive environment since I was born. My parents were also quite focused on education. Here's a pretty funny story… 

# Mostly About My Parents

It was 2014 and I was in middle school and I was a pretty fast runner, competing in 100m dash. I was on a team of 4 and we each ran 100m for a total 400m (one lap around a standard track). We won the city level race for us middle schoolers, and the other 3 were down to compete at the next level which would be like the county--Los Angeles county. But my parents already signed me up for the SATs the same day, so instead of helping my team out, I took the SATs as a middle schooler. To this day, it actually makes no sense whatsoever to devote my time taking an exam that has little impact on the rest of my life. It's funny though cuz it just shows how competitive my parents are in this community of Asians. Here's another example: (you can always close this whenever you want).

I had a lot of tutors when I was in middle school and highschool. And it's always so interesting to see how cutthroat my parents were about these tutors. Like, I remember my dad telling me paraphrased "you better not be telling other people about your tutors." Goddamn… it's not like highschool classes are curved or so difficult as to make it *that* important… honestly telling other people about these dudes could've helped me have more friends… anyways again I'm showing the kinds of things I lived through. I will be eternally grateful for the education I received, but I will not be happy with how cutthroat my parents were and how they did things. Here's a more specific example of this: 

A friend of mine invited my family to hang out with his family. I think they just wanted to talk and hang out… but my parents thought they had an ulterior motive and wanted to extract valuable information from us. Huhhh?? Like no? Another example,

My parents can't control their own lives, so they have to control mine. So whenever I make friends with new people, my parents are always capable of hating them. Without fail. It's pretty impressive. In middle school, boom--dont hang out with these guys. In high school, boom--dont hang out with these guys. In college, boom--dont hang out with these guys. "They're a bad influence." Damn, bro, you would like that after putting so much money into your kid, that kid would be able to make intelligent decisions about the people he hangs out with.

# More About Me

Let's move on to my motivation. In highschool, I took an extra AP class called AP Computer Science, during my second year, and here, I realized how fun computer science was. I would have to come to school at 7:00AM to take this class as a 0th period, because my schedule couldn't include it at any other time. Still, I remember how fun it was to solve problems in Java and BlueJay and the kinds of analytical thinking we would have to do.

Fast forward a few years, my favorite math teacher/tutor said "oh are you going to do computer science?" My response was that I was going to do biology/biochemistry so that I could become a doctor... but I always think back and wonder... if my parents weren't there to keep shoving me towards medicine, what would I want to do? What can be done at the intersection of computer science and medicine?

Fast forward a few more years and now I learn about the kind of new technology being made: ethereum, bitcoin, react, angular, etc. and now it appears like every day computer science is trying something new to improve on how we live our lives. It's pretty cool and I wanted to be a part of it. So... that is why I made this blog. I'm gonna try some stuff out and see if it'll be helpful for others along the same path.
